Roboticists Reportedly Working On Self-Driving Friend

   Roboticists at the University of Rochester are working on a highly advanced self-driving friend, anonymous inside sources reported to us earlier this week.

   “Using supervised learning techniques and an advanced forced-attention model, we’re getting extremely close to a friend who can handle their own errands and commute completely autonomously,” our source reported.

   Self-driving friends have long been an unattainable goal of automotive research, and many are questioning whether these friends will be fully capable of handling trickier situations on the road.

Search Continues For Student Lost In Random Forest

   Officials have begun the fourth day of the search for University of Rochester student Kendall Meyers, who disappeared early Saturday morning into a random forest she was training to assist doctors with medical diagnoses.

   “She was so bright and so talented,” recalls one of Meyers’s colleagues, who asked to remain anonymous.

   “She was so absorbed in her work, and ultimately, she got lost in it.”

   Department officials recommend exercising great care when working with random forests, not only due to the ease of getting lost, but also due to dangerous bugs found within.
