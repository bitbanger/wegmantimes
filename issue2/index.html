Professor Criswell Traded To Golden State Warriors

   URCS announced this week that Assistant Professor John Criswell will be traded to the Golden State Warriors, effective Fall 2018.

   This historic deal marks the first instance in which a tenured computer science professor has been traded to a professional sports league.

   “I’m very excited to take this new challenge head on,” Dr. Criswell said in a press conference this morning, “and I have complete confidence that Mr. Curry will do a fantastic job teaching the Operating Systems course.”

Haskell Legalized For Recreational Use On Campus

   URCS department chair Sandhya Dwarkadas signed an executive order early Monday morning to permit full use of Haskell as a recreational programming language on the University of Rochester campus.

   “More and more students have been experimenting with Haskell,” Dwarkadas told a Wegman Times reporter in an exclusive interview, “and we would rather those experiments take place on the department’s servers than on some unregulated laptop.”

   Although Haskell will be made recreationally legal “as soon as is functionally possible,” according to the department, using it to treat memory issues and harmful side effects of imperative code will remain forbidden indefinitely.
