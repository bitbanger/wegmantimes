Association of Computer Pseudoscientists Rejects Genetic Algorithms

   The Association of Computer Pseudoscientists issued a report this week suggesting that genetic algorithms could not explain the positive results they have indeed yielded, advocating instead for a “creationist” theory of optimization.

   “It is simply inconceivable that problems in such complex domains as radiography, locomotion, and and optics could be solved by a bunch of mutations,” according to a spokesperson for the ACP. “How could such intricate solutions have evolved from random numbers? It’s absurd.”

   Despite their often outlandish findings, the ACP is well respected by the systems and theory communities for their contributions to water-based memory and all-natural logarithms.
